## What Wholesaling Is (Quick Primer)

Wholesaling real estate means finding a property owner who needs to sell fast, getting a purchase contract at a below-market price, then *assigning* that contract to a cash buyer for a fee — typically $3,000–$15,000. You never own the property. You never need a mortgage. You need:

1. A motivated seller
2. A cash buyer
3. A contract (free template online or from a local real estate attorney — ~$200 one-time)

The margin comes from the spread between what the seller accepts and what the buyer pays.

## STEP 3: Qualify the Seller

Before you spend time on a deal, confirm three things:

1. **Motivation** — WHY do they want to sell? (Job loss, divorce, relocation, inheritance, foreclosure, tired landlord?)
2. **Timeline** — How quickly do they need to close?
3. **Price** — What's the minimum they'd accept?

If they're motivated, need to sell within 30–60 days, and have a realistic price, you have a deal worth pursuing.

**The magic question:** *"If I could give you all cash and close in 2 weeks with zero hassle, what's the lowest number you'd accept?"*

Get them to name it first. Whatever they say, respond: *"Let me run some numbers and I'll come back to you — is [slightly lower number] possible if we close fast?"*

## STEP 4: Run Your Numbers (The MAO Formula)

**Maximum Allowable Offer (MAO):**

```
MAO = (ARV × 0.70) – Repair Costs – Your Assignment Fee
```

- **ARV** = After Repair Value (what the house will be worth fixed up — use Zillow comps or Redfin)
- **0.70** = The 70% rule (leaves room for the buyer to profit and cover closing costs)
- **Repair Costs** = Estimate from a walkthrough (or use $15–$25/sqft for light rehab, $30–$50 for heavy)
- **Your Assignment Fee** = $3,000–$10,000 (build this into the formula)

**Example:**
- ARV: $280,000
- Repairs: $25,000
- Your fee: $7,000
- MAO: ($280,000 × 0.70) – $25,000 – $7,000 = **$164,000**

If the seller accepts $164,000 and your buyer pays $171,000, you pocket $7,000 at closing.

## STEP 7: Assign the Contract

Once you have a buyer:

1. Complete an **Assignment of Contract** agreement (also free on BiggerPockets/eForms)
2. The buyer pays your assignment fee at closing — typically via the title company
3. The title company handles the rest: title search, closing docs, fund transfer
4. You collect your check. You never owned the property.

**Use a local title company that's "investor-friendly"** — meaning they're comfortable with assignment contracts. Call ahead and ask: *"Are you familiar with wholesale assignments and double closes?"* If yes, you've found your title company.

## Legal Notes

- Wholesaling is legal in UT, CO, and ID without a real estate license **as long as you're selling your *contract*, not the property**
- If you're marketing properties you don't own (acting as an agent), you need a license
- Stick to marketing your equitable interest in the contract — you're fine
- When in doubt, consult a local real estate attorney for a one-hour consult (~$150–$250)
