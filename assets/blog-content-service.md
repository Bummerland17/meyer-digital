## How to Deliver

### Monthly Workflow (per client)

**Week 1:**
1. Review client's service list, recent jobs, and any seasonal angles
2. Pick 4 topics using Google autocomplete + "People Also Ask" for their main keywords
3. Generate posts with AI using your brand voice prompt
4. Review + lightly edit each post (5–10 min each)
5. Add a local hook (city name, neighborhood, local reference)
6. Drop finished posts in a shared Google Doc folder

**Week 2–4:**
- Client reviews at their pace (or you just publish if they've approved that)
- You publish to their CMS if they're on the publishing add-on

**Delivery format:**
- Shared Google Drive folder: `[Client Name] Blog — [Month Year]`
- Each post is its own Google Doc
- Include: Post title, meta description (for SEO), suggested publish date, one image keyword suggestion

**Google Doc template per post:**
```
Title: [SEO Title Here]
Meta Description: [155 characters, includes main keyword]
Target Keyword: [primary keyword]
Suggested URL slug: /blog/[keyword-slug]
---
[Post body here — ~700 words]
---
Image suggestion: Search "[keyword] stock photo" on Unsplash or Pexels
```

## Sample Blog Post Outline: Plumbing Company

**Business:** Jackson Hole Plumbing & Drain (hypothetical)  
**Target keyword:** "frozen pipes Jackson Wyoming"  
**Month:** November (pre-winter seasonal)

---

### Title: *"How to Prevent Frozen Pipes This Winter in Jackson Hole (And What to Do If It's Already Too Late)"*

**Meta description:** Jackson Hole winters are brutal on your plumbing. Here's exactly how to prevent frozen pipes — and who to call if one bursts.

---

**Introduction (~100 words)**
- Hook: "At -20°F, your pipes don't get a warning. They just burst."
- Establish stakes: water damage, emergency repair costs, insurance headaches
- Quick preview of what the post covers

**Section 1: Why Jackson Hole Is Especially High-Risk (~150 words)**
- Temperature extremes in Teton County vs. average Wyoming cities
- Old housing stock in East Jackson and surrounding areas
- Vacation homes left unoccupied for weeks during shoulder seasons
- Mention specific neighborhoods/areas if relevant (e.g., Wilson, Hoback)

**Section 2: 5 Ways to Prevent Frozen Pipes Before Winter Hits (~200 words)**
1. Insulate exposed pipes in crawl spaces, garages, and exterior walls
2. Set your thermostat no lower than 55°F — even when away
3. Open cabinet doors under sinks on exterior walls during cold snaps
4. Disconnect garden hoses and shut off exterior faucets
5. Know where your main shutoff valve is before you need it

**Section 3: Signs a Pipe Is Already Frozen (~100 words)**
- No water coming from faucet
- Visible frost on pipes
- Unusual smells from faucets
- What NOT to do (open flame — house fires happen)

**Section 4: What to Do When a Pipe Bursts (~100 words)**
- Shut off main water immediately
- Call a licensed plumber (CTA: link to their phone number)
- Document damage for insurance
- Don't wait — water damage compounds fast

**Conclusion + CTA (~80 words)**
- "Jackson Hole winters are serious. Your plumbing should be ready."
- CTA: "Call [Business Name] at [phone] for a free winterization check — we're local, we know this weather, and we're available 24/7 for emergencies."
