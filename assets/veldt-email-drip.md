**Preview text:** It's not the AI. It's the design.

---

Hey [First Name],

Three days ago you joined the Veldt waitlist. I want to share the thinking behind why I'm building this.

The problem isn't that LLMs aren't powerful enough.

GPT-4, Claude, Gemini — these models are genuinely impressive. They can reason, write, code, analyze, explain. The capability is there.

The problem is what everyone's building *on top* of them.

**Every AI "assistant" app works like this:**

You open it. You provide context (again). You ask a question or give a task. It responds. You go act on that response yourself. You close the app.

Repeat next session, starting from zero.

That workflow has AI doing the thinking and humans doing the execution. That's backwards.

**Here's what actually drove me insane:**

I manage contracts, vendors, investors, and team members across multiple businesses. The AI tool I wanted would:

- Know that Jake is my contractor and we have a standing weekly check-in
- Automatically flag when his invoice is late based on our usual timeline
- Draft *and send* the follow-up email when I ask it to
- Remember that I prefer Loom videos over calls for async updates
- Alert me if a business situation I'm tracking changes, without me asking again

I shouldn't have to explain any of that. Every single time. In every single session.

No tool does this. They all stop at "here's what I'd do if you let me."

**That gap is Veldt.**

Memory that persists. Actions that execute. Services that connect. An AI that works for you, not just with you.

I'll share more about how we're building this in a few days.
