# Lead Magnet Strategy — PantryMate

## Core Concept: "The No-Grocery-Run Dinner Guide"

**Full name:** *The No-Grocery-Run Dinner Guide: 10 Complete Meals From Pantry Staples You Already Own*

**Format:** PDF, 8–12 pages, well-designed (use Canva — free)
**Cost to create:** $0
**Cost to distribute:** $0
**Time to create:** 3–4 hours

---

## Why This Works

- Directly solves the same problem PantryMate solves (dinner without a shopping trip)
- Positions PantryMate as the logical "upgrade" at the end
- High perceived value — people love free recipe guides
- Pinterest loves this content (saves = free traffic forever)
- Facebook groups eat this up
- Can be gated behind an email opt-in (grow your list) OR used as a viral hook (free download, no email required — drives brand awareness)

**Recommendation:** Start with free, no-email-required. Get shares. Add email gate after you have social proof that people want it.

## Where to Distribute (Free, $0)

### Pinterest (Highest ROI for food content)
- Create a Pinterest pin with an eye-catching food photo + text overlay: "10 dinners from pantry staples — free guide 👇"
- Link pin to pantrymate.net or a landing page
- Use boards: "Meal Planning", "Budget Meals", "Quick Dinners", "Pantry Recipes"
- Pinterest content has a 6–12 month shelf life (vs. Twitter's 20 minutes)
- **Pro tip:** Create 5–10 different pin designs for the same guide. More pins = more discovery.

### Facebook Groups (High engagement, zero cost)
Target groups to post in:
- **Meal Prep Sunday** — search Facebook, multiple groups with 100K–500K members
- **Budget Meal Planning / Eating Well on a Budget** — 200K+ combined
- **Frugal Living** groups — 100K–500K members each
- **Zero Waste Living** groups — 50K–200K members
- **Busy Moms / Working Moms** groups — massive audience, meal planning is top pain point

**How to post without getting banned:**
- Be genuinely helpful, not spammy
- Comment in the group for a few days before posting
- Lead with the value: "I made a free guide for the group — 10 dinners from pantry staples most of us already have"
- Don't lead with the product, lead with the lead magnet

### Nextdoor
- Create a post in your local neighborhood (or US neighborhood if using VPN / US account)
- "Made a free meal planning guide for the community — 10 dinners from pantry staples, no shopping needed"
- Works surprisingly well for food/home content

### Indie Hackers / Hacker News
- Share the lead magnet as a launch milestone: "I made a free resource to go with my product launch"
- HN appreciates genuine value — a well-made free PDF + honest founder story can get traction

### Email signature
- Add to any email you send: "P.S. I made a free guide — 10 dinners from pantry staples → pantrymate.net/free-guide"

**Email 1 (Immediate):** Deliver the PDF
- Subject: "Your No-Grocery-Run Dinner Guide is here 🍳"
- Body: Link to PDF + 1 sentence about PantryMate

**Email 2 (Day 3):** Soft pitch
- Subject: "Quick question about dinner…"
- Body: "Did any of those recipes work? By the way — PantryMate does this for whatever's actually in YOUR fridge. Try it free."

**Email 3 (Day 7):** Social proof + CTA
- Subject: "The #1 thing people waste in their fridge (and how to fix it)"
- Body: Food waste stat → PantryMate solution → link
