## The Two Concepts

### Option A: "Wyoming Wild" 🏔️
**Niche:** Travel, food, outdoor life in Wyoming — specifically Jackson Hole, Grand Teton, Yellowstone corridor  
**Tie-in:** Directly supports local business network. You know the area. You can name-drop real places, real trails, real restaurants. That's an edge most faceless channels don't have.  
**Audience:** People planning Wyoming/Jackson Hole trips, outdoor enthusiasts, foodies, travel dreamers  
**Tone:** Adventure-forward, cinematic, practical ("what no one tells you about...")

### Option B: "Dinner Decided" 🍽️
**Niche:** Pantry cooking, meal planning from what you have, easy weeknight dinners  
**Tie-in:** Perfect PantryMate integration. Every video can end with "...and if you want to stop staring at your pantry confused, check the link below." Natural, not forced.  
**Audience:** Busy adults, budget-conscious home cooks, people who hate meal planning  
**Tone:** Practical, a little funny, feels like a helpful friend — not a food influencer

---

## Recommendation

**Start with "Dinner Decided."** Here's why:
- PantryMate affiliate angle is built-in from video 1
- Pantry/meal content has *massive* evergreen search volume (people Google this daily)
- Lower production bar — no need for Wyoming b-roll, just food visuals (Pexels/Pixabay have tons)
- Easier to batch 4–6 scripts in a sitting
- Scales to sponsorships faster (food brands, meal kit companies, kitchen tools)

**Run "Wyoming Wild" as channel #2** once Dinner Decided hits 1K subs, using some of the same workflow.

**Item 2:**
> *(Same format)*

**Item 3, 4, 5:**
> *(Same format — keep it moving, one visual per beat)*

---

**[PANTRYMATE PLUG — 4:00 to 4:20]**
> *(Light transition. Tone stays casual, not salesy.)*

Voiceover:
> "By the way — if you're tired of standing in front of your pantry with no idea what to make, there's an app called PantryMate that actually solves this. You log what you have, and it tells you what you can cook. It's free to try — link is in the description."

**[OUTRO — 4:20 to 5:00]**

**Script Notes:**
- Total word count for 5 min voiceover: ~700–800 words (at 150 WPM)
- ElevenLabs voice: Pick one and stick with it (consistency = brand)
- Recommended voices: "Adam" (calm, trustworthy), "Bella" (warm, friendly), or custom clone
- Generate audio first, then match B-roll to the narration timing

## Monetization Path

### Phase 1: Build (0–500 subscribers)
- **No monetization yet** — focus on publishing consistency and watch time
- Start PantryMate affiliate link in every description from video 1
- Expected affiliate revenue: $0–$20/mo (early days)

### Phase 2: Growing (500–1,000 subscribers)
- Apply for YouTube Partner Program at 1,000 subs + 4,000 watch hours
- Keep affiliate links active
- Estimated monthly: **$20–$50 AdSense** + **$10–$30 affiliate**
- First $100/mo milestone typically happens here

### Phase 3: Monetized (1,000+ subscribers, YPP approved)
- AdSense RPM for food/cooking content: $3–$8 per 1,000 views
- At 10,000 views/mo: **$30–$80/mo AdSense**
- Affiliate: scales with views — target $50–$150/mo
- Start pitching **local Wyoming business sponsorships** (for Wyoming Wild channel, or crossover content)
- Example pitch: "Wyoming Distillery sponsors a 'pantry cocktail' video — $150–$300 one-off"

### Phase 4: Scaling ($500+/mo)
- Mid-roll ads enabled (2x ad revenue)
- Cooking tool/kitchen brand sponsorships ($200–$500/video)
- PantryMate could become a direct partner deal (rev share or flat fee)
- Wyoming Wild channel running in parallel: local tourism board sponsorships, Jackson Hole outfitters, STR hosts

## Tools Stack

### Audio (You Already Have)
- **ElevenLabs** ✅ — Voiceover generation. Use one consistent voice ID per channel. Script → generate → download MP3.

### Video Editing
- **CapCut** (free, desktop) — Drag voiceover in, drop B-roll over it, add captions. Easiest learning curve.
- **DaVinci Resolve** (free) — More power, steeper learning curve. Better for long-term.

### Free Stock Footage
- **Pexels.com** — Best for food, kitchen, lifestyle. Huge library.
- **Pixabay.com** — Good backup, also free commercial use.
- **Coverr.co** — High-quality, curated. Great for hero shots.
- **Life of Vids** — Specifically good for outdoor/nature (Wyoming Wild channel)
- **Videvo.net** — Mixed free/paid; filter by free license

### Thumbnails
- **Canva** (free) — Create a template once, swap text/image per video. Batch thumbnails in 30 minutes.

### Script Writing
- **Claude or ChatGPT** — Paste your template, give the topic, get a first draft in 2 minutes
- Your job: edit for voice, add personal touches, punch up the hook

### Upload & SEO
- **TubeBuddy** (free tier) — Keyword research, tag suggestions, title scoring
- **VidIQ** (free tier) — Competitor analysis, trending topics

### Tracking
- **YouTube Studio** — Watch time, CTR, subscriber growth. Check weekly, not daily.

## Weekly Workflow (1 Video/Week)

| Day | Task | Time |
|---|---|---|
| Monday | Write script (AI-assisted) + review | 30 min |
| Tuesday | Generate ElevenLabs voiceover + collect B-roll | 30 min |
| Wednesday | Edit in CapCut, add captions, export | 45–60 min |
| Thursday | Design thumbnail in Canva, write title + description + tags | 20 min |
| Friday | Schedule upload for Saturday or Sunday (best engagement days) | 5 min |

**Total per video: ~2–2.5 hours.** That's it.
