### LinkedIn Post 3 — The Memory Problem

Here's something that would never be acceptable in any other product:

Every time you open your email, your contacts are gone.
Every time you reopen Spotify, your playlists reset.
Every time you start Word, your documents vanish.

No one would tolerate this. It's obviously broken.

Yet somehow, every AI assistant works this way.

You spend 10 minutes giving ChatGPT context on a project. Next session: gone. You have to start over. Every single time.

I manage multiple businesses. My context is deep. I have partners, ongoing deals, specific preferences, recurring workflows. I can't re-explain all of that every single day.

The "memory" features that exist right now are a band-aid. They're clunky, hit-or-miss, and don't actually reason over your past interactions intelligently.

Veldt is being built memory-first.

It knows who you are. What you care about. What's in progress. It builds a model of *you* — and it uses that model to be actually useful, not just politely responsive.

Persistent, structured, intelligent memory. Not a cute feature. The foundation.

Building in public. This is what we're working on.

### Title: I'm building an AI assistant that actually does things. Here's the frustration that started it.

**The problem I kept hitting:**

I run multiple businesses. I'm based in Namibia, but my companies operate in Wyoming. Everything I do is remote, async, and AI-assisted.

Or at least — it's supposed to be.

Here's what actually happens: I ask my AI assistant to do something. It tells me what it *would* do. Then I go do it. Every time. Without fail.

I needed to follow up with a contractor. My "AI assistant" drafted an email and waited for me to click send.

I wanted to track a specific business situation and be alerted if it changed. It told me to set a Google Alert.

I asked it to remember that my investor likes Thursday afternoon calls. Next session: had to tell it again.

This is the pattern. You get a very capable brain that is completely passive. And at some point I realized: this isn't what "assistant" means. An assistant acts. Mine just answers.

**What I tried:**

- ChatGPT (all tiers, including Teams)
- Claude
- Notion AI
- Various "AI productivity" apps (Mem, Magical, you name it)
- Custom GPTs, custom Claude projects
- Zapier + AI combos
- Building my own with LangChain

Some of this is genuinely useful. None of it solved the core problem: AI that remembers you, monitors things, and takes actions without you having to drive every single step.

**What I'm building:**

Veldt. A personal AI assistant with three core pillars:

**1. Real persistent memory**
Not a list of facts. A model of you — your people, your projects, your preferences, your context. Used intelligently in every interaction.

**2. Real integrations**
Not just reading your email. Actually sending it. Actually scheduling meetings. Actually writing to your CRM. The AI has to be able to touch your real systems.

**3. Proactive actions**
Standing instructions that execute. "Follow up in 3 days." "Alert me when X happens." "Every week, do Y." It does these things. You don't re-prompt.

**Where I am:**

Pre-launch. Building. The waitlist is live.

I'm a solo founder using AI agents to build AI products — which is both the appropriate way to build this and genuinely productive. I'm moving fast.

**Traction so far:**

Waitlist just opened at https://bummerland17.github.io/veldt-ai/ — building the list now. If you've felt this frustration, get on it. Early members get founding pricing and first access.

**Why I think this wins:**

Everyone building "AI assistants" right now is building chat interfaces on top of LLMs. That's fine. But the category that actually matters — AI that operates autonomously on your behalf — is mostly unsolved at the product level. The tech is ready. The product design isn't.

That's the gap I'm going after.

**The name:**

Veldt is the Afrikaans/Dutch word for open grassland. I'm from Namibia. It's where the name and the brand come from — earthy, premium, built on something real.
