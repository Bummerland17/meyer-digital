## Target Audience Definition

### Primary Audience
**"The Exhausted Home Cook"**
- **Age:** 28–45
- **Gender:** Skews female (65/35 F/M) but don't exclude men
- **HHI:** $40k–$100k (broad — food waste affects all income levels)
- **Life stage:** Busy professionals, parents with kids at home, couples, single adults with full-time jobs
- **Location:** US, Canada, Australia, UK (English-speaking, Western food waste culture)
- **Device:** iPhone-heavy (iOS users convert better for apps)

### Psychographics & Pain Points
- Feels guilty throwing away food but keeps doing it
- Runs out of dinner ideas by Wednesday
- Wants to eat healthier but defaults to takeout when tired
- Cares about saving money but doesn't track it consciously
- Overwhelmed by meal planning — too much friction to do it weekly
- Secretly embarrassed by fridge full of forgotten vegetables

### Facebook/Instagram Interest Targeting
**Layer 1 (Behaviors):**
- Engaged shoppers (online)
- Cooking apps users
- Health & wellness app users

**Layer 2 (Interests):**
- Cooking, recipes, meal prep
- Food Network, Tasty, Bon Appétit
- Whole Foods, Trader Joe's, Instacart
- HelloFresh, Blue Apron (competitor crossover — they're already paying for food convenience)
- Budget living, frugal living, YNAB

**Layer 3 (Demographics):**
- Parents of children under 18
- Homeowners
- College educated

### Lookalike Seed Audiences
- Your app installer list (once you have 100+ installs)
- Email list from waitlist/signups
- Top 25% LTV customers (when available)
- Video viewers 50%+ on best-performing ad

## Five Ad Angles

### Angle 1: The Shame Hook (Problem/Agitation)
**Hook:** *"You have $60 of food in your fridge. You just opened DoorDash."*
**Body:** Every week, the average American family throws away $30 in groceries. That's $1,500 a year — wasted. PantryMate's AI scans what you have and tells you exactly what to cook tonight. No planning. No waste. Just dinner.
**CTA:** Download Free — Cook Smarter Tonight
**Why it works:** Opens a guilt loop instantly. Relatable to 90% of the audience. Specific dollar amount creates emotional resonance.

---

### Angle 2: The Speed Hook (Before/After)
**Hook:** *"30 seconds to know what's for dinner — using food you already have."*
**Body:** Stop staring at the fridge. PantryMate scans your ingredients and suggests 3 meals you can make right now. No grocery trip. No subscription box. Free.
**CTA:** Try PantryMate Free
**Why it works:** Addresses the specific moment of frustration (5pm, staring at fridge). Speed promise is tangible.

---

### Angle 3: The Money Angle (Financial Pain)
**Hook:** *"I saved $200 last month just by actually using my groceries."*
**Body:** UGC-style testimonial. Person shows their fridge, scans with PantryMate, sees meal suggestions pop up. "I used to throw out half my produce. Now I cook 4x a week using what I already bought." Download free below.
**CTA:** Save Money on Food — Free App
**Why it works:** Concrete dollar saving. UGC format performs well in feed. Shareable.

---

### Angle 4: The Curiosity/Intelligence Angle (AI Positioning)
**Hook:** *"This AI looked at my fridge and suggested a better dinner than I ever would have."*
**Body:** Most recipe apps tell you to go buy more ingredients. PantryMate works backwards — scan what you have, and it builds meals around your actual kitchen. It's how a personal chef would think.
**CTA:** Download PantryMate — It's Free
**Why it works:** Positions against recipe apps directly. "Personal chef" aspiration. AI novelty still converts.

---

### Angle 5: The Emotional Payoff (Identity/Aspirational)
**Hook:** *"I stopped feeling like a bad cook. Turns out I just needed better suggestions."*
**Body:** Cooking confidence is real. When you know what to make — and you already have everything you need — cooking stops feeling hard. PantryMate gives you that clarity every night.
**CTA:** Find Your Inner Chef — Free Download
**Why it works:** Speaks to shame/identity around cooking. Empowering rather than problem-focused. Strong for retargeting warm audiences.

## Hook Formulas That Work for Meal Planning Apps

### Pattern Interrupt Hooks
*Designed to stop the scroll with something unexpected*
- "I threw away $1,500 worth of food last year. And I meal-planned."
- "Your fridge is a $200 meal kit you forgot you bought."
- "The best meal planning app doesn't give you recipes — it reads your fridge."

### Stat-Based Hooks
*Use specificity to create credibility and scale of problem*
- "The average family wastes 31% of the food they buy. Every. Single. Week."
- "Americans spend $218B/year on food they never eat. This app fixes that."
- "Dinner in 30 seconds. No, really — I timed it."

### Pain Point Hooks
*Meet the audience at their exact moment of frustration*
- "It's 5:30pm. You're tired. What's for dinner? (You already have the answer.)"
- "I used to spend 20 minutes deciding and then order pizza anyway."
- "Full fridge. No ideas. Every. Single. Night."

### Curiosity Hooks
*Create an information gap the viewer must close*
- "I asked an AI what to cook with my leftovers. It was shockingly good."
- "There's an app that tells you what to cook based on what's actually in your kitchen."
- "What if your fridge could talk? (Kind of.)"

## Budget Strategy: $10/Day

### Phase 1: Testing (Days 1–14) — $10/day
**Goal:** Find your winning ad + audience combo
- Run 3–4 ad variations across 2 ad sets
- Ad Set A: Interest-based (cooking + budget + HelloFresh crossover)
- Ad Set B: Broad (age 28–44 US, no interest filters — let Meta optimize)
- Kill any ad with CTR below 1% after $20 spend
- Kill any audience with CPC above $3.00 after $30 spend
- Budget split: $5/day per ad set

### Phase 2: Iteration (Days 15–30) — $10/day
**Goal:** Double down on winner, test new angles
- Pause losers, put $8/day behind winning ad set
- Use $2/day to test 1 new ad angle
- Start building your retargeting audience (video viewers, link clickers)

### Phase 3: Scale Signal (Day 30+)
- If CPI < $3.00: double budget to $20/day, watch ROAS
- If retargeting pool > 500 users: launch $2/day retargeting campaign
- If you have 100+ installs: create Lookalike Audience

## Realistic Performance Benchmarks

| Metric | Conservative | Target | Best Case |
|--------|-------------|--------|-----------|
| **CPM** | $12–18 | $8–12 | $6–8 |
| **CTR** | 0.8–1.2% | 1.5–2.5% | 3%+ |
| **CPC** | $1.00–2.00 | $0.50–1.00 | $0.25–0.50 |

| **Installs/day @ $10** | 2–4 | 4–8 | 8–15 |

*CPM and CPC vary significantly by time of year, audience competition, and creative quality. These benchmarks are for US iOS, consumer app category, cold traffic.*

### Statistical Significance
- You need **~50 installs per ad variation** to have directional signal
- At $2.50 CPI, that's ~$125 per variation
- With 3 variations: ~$375 before you have real data
- At $10/day: expect 2–3 weeks before you can make confident decisions
