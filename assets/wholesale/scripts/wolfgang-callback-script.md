# Wolfgang's Personal Callback Script
## For Calling Warm Seller Leads Rex Has Already Qualified

---

> **When to use this:** Rex flagged this lead as warm — the seller answered, confirmed ownership, and expressed openness to a cash offer. Now it's your turn. You're the decision-maker. Be human, be direct, be helpful.

---

## BEFORE THE CALL — REVIEW REX'S NOTES

Check the lead in your deal tracker:
- What's the address?
- What's the seller's name?
- What did Rex learn about motivation, condition, and price expectation?
- Any red flags?

---

## THE CALL

### OPENING

> "Hey [SELLER NAME], this is Wolfgang — Wolfgang Meyer. My assistant Rex reached out to you earlier about your property. I'm the actual investor behind the operation. Do you have a few minutes to chat?"

---

### IF THEY REMEMBER REX

> "Good, glad Rex was helpful. So I understand you have a property at [ADDRESS] you might be considering selling — I wanted to reach out personally. Tell me a bit about what's going on with it..."

*[Let them talk. People want to be heard. Don't interrupt. Take notes.]*

---

### IF THEY DON'T REMEMBER / ARE COLD

> "No worries — Rex reached out about the property at [ADDRESS]. We're investors in the Phoenix area and we do direct cash purchases — no agents, no listings, no hassle. I just wanted to introduce myself and see if a cash offer would even make sense for your situation."

---

### KEY QUESTIONS TO CONFIRM (even if Rex got them)

**Condition:**
> "Just so I can get a rough sense of the numbers — what kind of shape is the property in? Are we talking mostly cosmetic stuff like paint and carpet, or are there bigger issues — roof, HVAC, foundation, anything like that?"

**Situation:**
> "And what's driving the timing for you? Like, are you on a deadline or is this more of a 'when the right offer comes' situation?"

**Price:**
> "Do you have a number in mind? Or are you still figuring that out?"
*(If they give a number that might work: "Okay, I can work with that range." If it's too high: "Hmm, that's a bit higher than we'd typically be — let me take a look at the comps and get back to you. Is that okay?")*

---

### MOVING TOWARD A VISIT / PHOTOS

> "Here's what I'd like to do — can I send someone by in the next day or two to take a quick look? It'd just be a 15-minute walkthrough, no obligation. Or if that's easier, could you send me a few photos of the main areas? I just need enough to put a number together."

---

### IF THEY WANT A PRICE NOW

> "I hear you — I don't want to waste your time. I'm pretty good at estimating, but I'd be doing you a disservice if I threw out a number without seeing the place and pulling some recent comps. Give me 24 hours after I see it — or after you send photos — and I'll have a real number for you. Fair?"

---

### MAKING THE OFFER (after you've run the numbers)

> "Okay, so I ran the numbers on [ADDRESS]. Based on the condition and what's been selling nearby, I'm able to offer $[YOUR OFFER]. Cash, close in 10-14 days, no agents, no repairs, I cover closing costs.
>
> I know that might be lower than what you were hoping for, but here's what you're getting: certainty. No deals falling through, no inspections coming back with a laundry list, no waiting 60 days for a bank. Just a clean, fast close."

---

### IF THEY ACCEPT (or are very interested)

> "Great. What I'll do is send you a simple one-page purchase agreement — it just locks in the price and the timeline. Nothing complicated. I'll send it over today via [email/DocuSign]. Take a look, and if you have questions just call me directly.
>
> One thing I always put in these contracts — it says 'Wolfgang Meyer or assigns.' That just means I might bring in a co-investor for the closing. It doesn't change anything for you — same price, same timeline. Does that make sense?"

### IF THEY NEED TIME TO THINK

> "Of course — it's a big decision. Here's what I'll do: I'll send you a quick follow-up text with my number so you have it. If you want to move forward, or if you just have questions, reach out anytime. I'm easy to get ahold of.
>
> Can I check back in with you in a couple days?"

---

### CLOSING THE CALL (any outcome)

> "I appreciate you taking the time, [NAME]. Whatever you decide, I hope things work out well for you."

## RUNNING THE NUMBERS — QUICK FORMULA

Ask your AI (me) for comps at the address. Then:

```
ARV × 0.70 = Maximum All-In Price (MAO)
MAO - Estimated Repairs = Your Max Offer to Seller
Your Max Offer to Seller + $5,000–$10,000 = Assignment Price to Buyer
```

**Example:**
- ARV: $250,000
- 70% of ARV: $175,000
- Estimated Repairs: $30,000
- Max offer to seller: $145,000
- You offer seller: $140,000 (leave room)
- You sell to buyer for: $150,000
- **Your assignment fee: $10,000** 🎉

---

## IMPORTANT REMINDERS

- You are a **private investor**, not a real estate agent. You're buying for yourself (or assigning your equitable interest).
- The "or assigns" language in the contract is what makes wholesale legal in Arizona.
- Never tell a seller you're going to "find them a buyer" — you're buying the property.
- If they ask about the assignment: "I may bring in a co-investor. Your deal stays exactly the same."
