### Post 1 — Tip Post: Documentation

**Hook:** One habit that saved me in a tenant dispute last year 👇

When my tenant claimed I "never fixed" the leaking faucet she complained about in October, I had a timestamp, her exact words, and the date I sent the plumber — all in one place.

Here's what most landlords skip: **document every maintenance request the moment it comes in.** Not in your texts. Not in your head. Somewhere with a timestamp you can actually pull up.

My process now:
- Every unit has its own link (I text it to tenants on move-in day)
- They submit requests through it — no account needed on their end
- I get a notification, it logs automatically, and I can see everything by unit

I've used it twice in 12 months to defend myself against "you never responded" complaints. Both times, the record was right there.

Whatever system you use — even a shared Google Sheet — just make sure it's timestamped and tied to the unit, not to a phone number.

Anyone else learned this the hard way? 👇

## 4. LinkedIn Strategy

---

### 5 Search Terms to Find Small Landlords

Use LinkedIn's People search with these keyword combinations:

1. `"rental property" "self-managed" owner`
2. `"landlord" "1-4 units" OR "duplex" OR "triplex"`
3. `"real estate investor" "buy and hold" "single family"`
4. `"accidental landlord" OR "part-time landlord"`
5. `"property owner" "tenant" NOT "property management company" NOT "commercial"`

**Tips:**
- Filter by geography (target mid-size cities with high rental activity)
- Filter by "1st and 2nd connections" first
- Look for people who list specific addresses or unit counts in their bios — that's a signal
