The problem with AI sales prompts

Most salespeople using AI are typing something like "write me a cold email to a CFO about our software." And they get back something that sounds like a software company wrote it, because that's exactly what it is.

The issue isn't the AI — it's the prompts. Vague inputs produce generic outputs. And generic sales emails get deleted without being read.

Good sales prompts need context variables, tone calibration, and a structure that mirrors how real sales conversations actually progress. That's not something you figure out in an afternoon.

What a real prompt looks like

Here's one from the cold outreach category (free to use):

Prompt #3 — Cold email: problem-first openerYou are a B2B salesperson writing a cold email. Do NOT mention our product in the first sentence. Context: - Prospect role: [JOB_TITLE] at [COMPANY_TYPE] - Common problem in their role: [SPECIFIC_PAIN_POINT] - Our solution: [ONE_SENTENCE_DESCRIPTION] - Tone: direct, peer-to-peer (not vendor-to-buyer) Write a 4-sentence cold email that: 1. Opens with the pain point (not "I hope this finds you well") 2. Asks one yes/no question about whether this is a real problem for them 3. Mentions our solution in one sentence only if the answer is yes 4. Ends with a low-commitment CTA (15-min call, not "schedule a demo")
