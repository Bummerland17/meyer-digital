Most indie launches fail for the same reason

The product works. The design is decent. The pricing is reasonable. But when launch day comes, the strategy is "post on Twitter and submit to ProductHunt" — and two days later there are 12 signups, 8 of which are the founder's friends.

This isn't because getting early users is hard. It's because the playbooks most people follow are written by people who already had an audience when they launched. "Build in public for 6 months" is good advice if you already have 5,000 Twitter followers. It's useless advice if you're starting from zero.

The honest version: Most channels don't work until you have social proof. The ones that work from day zero are community-based — specific Reddit threads, niche Discord servers, targeted cold DMs to people who've publicly said they have the problem you solve. This playbook is about those channels.
