## ✅ WHAT'S ACTUALLY WORKING IN 2025

### The Truth Engine: Acquisition Strategies Ranked by ROI

#### 1. 🔥 Cold Calling (Still #1 for Speed)
**What:** Skip trace distressed property owners → call directly  
**Response Rate:** ~1–3% contact-to-lead; 164% better on pre-foreclosure within 24hrs of filing  
**Cost:** $5–15/hr for VAs (Philippines/offshore), skip trace ~$0.10–0.30/record  
**Why It Works:** Creates instant human connection — texts and email are ignored  
**Script Hook (Opening):**
> "Hey [First Name], my name is [Your Name]. I'm reaching out about a property I believe you own at [123 Main Street]. Did I catch you at a bad time?"

**Key Insight:** Multi-channel follow-up increases conversion **287%** vs single-channel. Call → text → mail sequence beats any single method.

**Best Seller Motivation Triggers:**
- Financial distress / pre-foreclosure
- Inherited property (not local, don't want hassle)
- Landlord tired of tenants
- Divorce / estate situations
- Property in disrepair / deferred maintenance

---

#### 2. 📬 Direct Mail (Best for Serious Seller Quality)
**Response Rate:** 1–3% general lists; 3.32% real estate average; up to 9% with targeted/personalized lists  
**Best Angle:** Outperforms ALL digital channels by 600% (Data & Marketing Association)  
**Cost:** $0.50–$1.00/piece, 500–1,000 piece minimum for meaningful data  
**What Works:**
- Personalized letters (name + address + specific situation reference)
- Handwritten yellow letters or typed, non-corporate appearance
- Personalization boosts open rates **135%** (Lob/Compu-Mail research)
- Multiple touches — send same list 3–5x before abandoning

**Best Lists:**
- Pre-foreclosure (NOD filings)
- Probate / estate properties
- Tax-delinquent properties
- Absentee owners (out-of-state owners, especially 5+ years)
- High equity owners (90%+ equity, owned 10+ years)

### Common Mistakes Wholesalers Make in 2025 (Avoid These)

1. **Overestimating ARV** — The killer mistake. Use 3 conservative comps, not 1 optimistic one
2. **No buyers before deals** — Sourcing deals without knowing who'll buy = stuck with unsellable contract
3. **Treating wholesaling as a side hustle** — No system = no consistency; treat as a business from Day 1
4. **Single-channel outreach** — Cold calling only, or mail only — multi-channel converts 287% better
5. **Not having proof of funds** — Serious sellers want to see POF, get a POF letter from a hard money lender
6. **Not maintaining a buyer list** — Should have 50+ active, vetted buyers per market minimum
7. **Ignoring disclosure requirements** — Pennsylvania (Act 52, Jan 2025), Connecticut, Maryland, others now require disclosures — know your state laws
8. **Misscoping rehabs** — "Lipstick and list it" projects sit. Buyers are picky in 2025. Price deals that support real rehabs
9. **Chasing hot markets blind** — Virtual wholesaling into Phoenix or Tampa without local contacts fails
10. **Not following up** — Research: calling pre-foreclosure leads within 24 hours = 164% higher contact rate

6. **Title Company Networking** — Call 3–4 local title companies, ask: *"Do you have any real estate investors you work with who might want off-market deals?"* — title companies love referring investors to wholesalers (more closings = more business for them).
7. **County Property Auctions** — Attend courthouse foreclosure sales. Everyone there is a buyer. Get cards.
8. **Contractor Referrals** — Call local contractors and ask: "Do you have any investor clients looking for properties to flip?" Contractors know every flipper in town.

### How to Match Deals to Buyer Criteria Before Locking Up a Contract

**The Wolfgang Method (Buyer-First Deal Sourcing):**

1. **Build your buyer matrix first** — Create a spreadsheet with each buyer's criteria:
   - Zip codes they buy
   - Max price
   - Property condition preference
   - Min # bedrooms
   - Target ARV range

2. **When a potential deal comes in — before signing the contract:**
   - Run quick ARV (3 comps, conservative)
   - Estimate repairs (use sq ft × $25 light / $40 medium / $65 heavy)
   - Calculate MAO: (ARV × 0.65) − Repairs = Your Max Offer
   - Your fee: ARV × 5–10% baked into the spread
   - Check your buyer matrix: Does anyone on your list buy in this zip at this price with this condition?

3. **Text/call your top 3 matching buyers BEFORE signing:**
   > *"Hey [buyer], I've got a [3/2 SFR] in [zip code]. ARV around [$185K], needs $25K work. I can get it at [$90K–$95K]. That in your wheelhouse? Would you close on it at [price + fee]?"*

4. **If 2+ buyers say yes → sign the contract**
   If zero buyers say yes → adjust your offer price or pass on the deal

5. **Assignment fee = difference between your contract price and what the buyer pays**
   - You contract at $90K → sell at $105K → $15K assignment fee

**Rule:** Never sign a purchase contract you can't exit. Always use assignable contracts with inspection periods.
