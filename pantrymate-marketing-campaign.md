## 2. Facebook Group Outreach Strategy

### Target Groups

| # | Group Name | Why It Fits | Post Angle |
|---|-----------|-------------|------------|
| 1 | **Meal Planning Mamas** (~500K members) | Core audience: family cooks overwhelmed by daily dinner decisions. High engagement, practical mindset. | "What do I make with what I have" is their #1 weekly complaint. Offer the solution framed as a time-saver for busy parents. |
| 2 | **Zero Waste Living** (~300K members) | Members hate wasting food. PantryMate directly reduces waste by using what's on hand. | Frame around: "I stopped throwing out food when I started doing this." Tie to sustainability, not convenience. |
| 3 | **Frugal Living & Saving Money** (~800K members) | Budget-conscious cooks who use every ingredient. Huge overlap with pantry-first cooking. | ROI angle: "Stopped buying groceries I didn't need. Here's how I use what I already have." |
| 4 | **Budget Bytes Community** (~200K members) | Fans of the budget cooking blog. Already primed for practical, no-frills meal ideas. | Complement their existing mindset: "Another tool for the no-waste, no-budget-drain kitchen." |
| 5 | **Family Freezer Meal Planning** (~150K members) | Batch cookers who care deeply about using ingredients efficiently. | Angle: "Before I batch cook, I run my ingredients through this to figure out what's worth doubling." |
| 6 | **Cooking for One or Two** (~120K members) | Singles and couples who hate waste from oversized recipes. Real pain: buying a whole head of cabbage and using 1/4. | Focus on the "use what you actually bought" problem. Very relatable. |
| 7 | **Plant-Based on a Budget** (~180K members) | Vegan/vegetarian cooks who rotate cheap staples (lentils, beans, rice). PantryMate handles these well. | Show a pantry-based plant meal generated from common staples. Proof > pitch. |
| 8 | **Instant Pot & Air Fryer Recipes** (~1M+ members) | Appliance-specific cooks — huge group, active, recipe-hungry. Many don't know what to make with their gadgets. | Angle: "I told an AI what I had and what appliances, it gave me an air fryer meal in 30 seconds." Hook on the device. |

## 3. Email Drip — Activation Sequence

*Send to free users who signed up but haven't upgraded. Keep it human. No "valued customer" language.*

---

**Subject:** You signed up. Did you actually try it?

Hey,

Thanks for joining PantryMate.

One thing I've noticed: people sign up and then forget to actually open the fridge and type stuff in. So — do that right now if you haven't.

Go to pantrymate.net, type whatever's in your fridge (even the sad stuff), and see what comes back. Takes 30 seconds.

If you hit a wall or it gives you something weird, hit reply and tell me. I read these.

— Wolfgang
Founder, PantryMate

*P.S. Free tier gives you a solid feel for it. Pro unlocks saved meals, dietary filters, and smarter suggestions if you want to go deeper.*

---

### Day 3 Email — Friction Removal / Feature Highlight

**Subject:** The thing most people don't know PantryMate does

Hey,

Quick one.

Most people use PantryMate like a search engine — type stuff, get a meal, done. That works.

But Pro users do something smarter: they set dietary preferences once (vegetarian, low-carb, dairy-free, whatever) and every suggestion auto-filters from there. No more ignoring half the results.

Also: meal history. So you're not getting suggested the same 4 things every week.

Free plan is good. Pro is for people who use it more than twice a week.

If that's you → pantrymate.net/upgrade

— Wolfgang
